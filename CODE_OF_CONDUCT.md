# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as contributors and maintainers pledge to make participation in TraceState a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:

- Being respectful and constructive in discussion.
- Focusing on repository quality, documentation, and technical accuracy.
- Accepting responsibility and learning from mistakes.
- Showing empathy toward other contributors and users.

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

- Harassment, intimidation, or discriminatory language.
- Personal attacks or deliberate disruption.
- Publishing private information without permission.
- Any conduct that would reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional open-source project.

## Enforcement

Project maintainers may remove, edit, or reject contributions and comments that do not align with this code of conduct.

## Scope

This code of conduct applies in all project spaces, including issues, pull requests, discussion threads, and any other public or private communication where the project is represented.
